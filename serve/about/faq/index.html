I signed up for PayAus. What’s next?

Once you sign up for PayAus, you’ll get a guided tour through the system. It will give you a brief introduction into how everything works. From there, the next steps are:

  1. Import your staff data from MYOB, Wage Easy, Excel, or a variety of other sources
  2. Introduce your staff to PayAus
  3. Get a terminal. We’ll contact you to arrange delivery of one, usually a few days after your demo. Contact us directly if you don’t want to wait.

I see “password” and “passcode” a lot. What’s the difference?

Your passcode is a numerical code — usually four digits — that’s used to clock into a hardware terminal or online portal. This is used to generate your timesheet

Your password is used in conjunction with your email to log in to www.payaus.com

You can see passcodes and passwords for your staff on their staff details page.

How do I change my password?

While logged in to PayAus, choose Change My Password from the menu in the top right corner.

How do I change my passcode?

If you’re logged in as a manager or organisation admin, you can change passcodes for your staff. Just edit the field on their staff details page.

If you’ve just forgotten your passcode, you can get it emailed to you by following the prompts from the online portal.

What shortcuts and abbreviations can I use?

You can use all sorts of abbreviations when entering times into timesheets and rosters (in week view or day view). For example, if you wanted to enter 3:30 PM, you could write it in any of the following ways — PayAus is smart enough to know what you mean.

15:30
15.30
15-30
1530
15 30
3:30pm
3,30 PM
3:30 p
330p

How about 9 AM? Try any of these:

9:00
9.00
9-00
9 00
900
9
9am
9 AM
9 a
9a

Remember: PayAus timesheets and rosters work in 24 hour time. Make sure you enter “5pm” (or “17:00”, etc.), not just “5” (or “5am”!), for afternoons.
