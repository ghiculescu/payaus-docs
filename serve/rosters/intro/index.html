Getting Started with Rosters

Got a question? Email us, check our FAQ, or tweet @PayAusPayroll for a quick answer.
Print this guide.
Last updated: Tuesday, 20 August, 2013

This PayAus user guide was downloaded from help.payaus.com/rosters/intro/

PayAus is great at telling you when your staff were at work, but it’s equally good for arranging when they should be there.

Here’s what a PayAus roster looks like, in week view:

Rosters are split in three views. We have a guide for each one, to help you build your rosters with ease. You can navigate between views using the headers at the top of the roster — click a date to see the roster for that day, Week View to see an overview of the week, or Costs to see a cost analysis for the roster.

  • Week View
  • Day View
  • Costs View

To help you design your rosters, you can add:

  • Tags

Once you’ve finished designing your roster, you can:

  • Share it by SMS or email
  • Print it

Rosters are weekly, and you can change the week you are looking at by clicking on the date displaying, then choosing a new week.

PayAus is a fully integrated solution for rostering, reporting and timesheeting.
